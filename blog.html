New York family adventure

Feb. 19th, 2016

My cousin Emily's quest for the perfect prom dress fabrics brought my mom and I, as well as my Aunt Leslie and cousin Alyssa, to New York City for an eventful weekend. The trip started with a long, cold, train ride, in the company of some colorful passengers. When we arrived in the city that evening, we were welcomed by an awesome sunset (framed perfectly from our hotel window in Time Square), a yummy seafood dinner, and a whimsical broadway showing of 'Finding Neverland.' Saturday started with a humbling visit to the 9/11 memorial followed by an afternoon spent at Mood Fabrics. With the help of my exquisite taste, Emily was able to pick out the most fantastic fabrics to create her masterpiece of a prom dress. The day ended with a classic Irish pub meal and pictures in Time Square.

Welcome, everyone!

Feb. 4th, 2016

Thank you for visiting my site; I’m even more appreciative you want to check out the blog… I’ll do my best to keep things interesting!

For this first entry, I thought it’d be beneficial for you all to get to know a bit more about me. Though the “about” section of the site has a quick synopsis of my story, that abridged version might not sell you on how cool I am (or more honestly, how weird I am). And hopefully it’ll become even more evident how much I’m in love with photography.

I have always been, undoubtedly, the artsy child. Whether that is a symptom of middle-child syndrome, a random-chance character trait, or a result of environmental influences (most likely a combination of the three…), I am a creative at my core. Contrastingly enough, I also enjoy science. Controlled environments and information backed with solid reasoning ARE.MY.THANG.

No boyfriend? No problem! Just make one out of a balloon and some construction paper.

By now, things should be making sense:
Creative person + Science = PHOTOGRAPHER!

Well, eight-year-old Tori had plans of being a Broadway Star. Thirteen-year-old Tori was bound to become a Comic book Illustrator. And college-bound eighteen-year-old Tori wasn’t about to be a starving artist, but instead, an environment-saving Biologist.

After attending Seton Hill University for two years where I studied biology, I transferred to Penn State University. There, I majored in environmental resource management. Let me tell you about those “ERM”-ers: they are some of the most passionate people I’ve ever met… And being around that kind of passion was eye opening; it was bittersweet to discover (admit) that I had been pursuing the wrong career all along, and with barely one semester left nonetheless.

With photography, so many things can be evoked (i.e. ideas, emotions, dancing… yes, I’ve found myself dancing after taking an awesome shot). And without getting too cliché, that’s what fuels me. Every shot evokes something different, not only with me, but with YOU! And nothing feels better than sharing that moment.

Here, I caught my grandparents silently reminiscing over an image, taken about 15 years ago, of themselves’ surrounded by all of their grandchildren. What’s especially emotional about this photograph is that their deceased grandson, my cousin, Eric, is pictured centered. Not only am I evoked emotionally by this brief moment of mourning; my grandparents were so strongly influenced by one simple picture.

Long story short, here I am attempting to make a career out of what I love to do. I plan on keeping you all entertained with eclectic blog posts to match my eclectic interests (if you stick around, please be prepared to see lots of images of insects and Beasley…)

Here’s that Beasley photo I promised!

So now that you’ve heard my story, let me help you capture yours!
